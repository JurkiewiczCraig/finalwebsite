Showcasing the EPA's Walkability Index

For Citizens and City Planners alike

Ideal

Create a user friendly interactive web platform that display and inform members of the public on factors that are involved with creating a pedestrian friendly walkable city

Reality

The general public and city planning staff don't have readily-accessible, user-friendly data when it comes to the factors that influence walkability

Consequences

If the public lacks knowledge of the factors that go into creating a pedestrian-friendly city, they wonâ€™t be able to offer useful feedback when city officials make or consider making changes to policy. As well, the city officials can't make accurate decisions without a full understanding of the factors that influence walkability in their area and a way to display those factors.

Proposal

To create interactive web applications that inform the public and city officials of factors that are taken into account when creating a walkability index. Some of these apps will be very user-friendly and focused on the public/less-informed city staff, while others will be more complex and detailed, with those being focused more towards city staff with experience in planning and some pre-existing knowledge of walkability.

Technology Explored

AGOL, ArcGIS Dashboards, Storymaps, Leaflet, Experience Builder
